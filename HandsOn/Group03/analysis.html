General analysis of the Dataset

1. Station Distribution

The dataset includes approximately 17 to 20 monitoring stations. Most are located in urban traffic (UT) zones, with a few situated in urban background (UF) or suburban (S) areas.

  • Plaza de España – Urban Traffic (UT)
  • Escuelas Aguirre – Urban Traffic (UT)
  • Casa de Campo – Suburban (S)

2. Measured Pollutants

The dataset records the following air pollutants:

  • NO2 – Nitrogen Dioxide
  • SO2 – Sulfur Dioxide
  • CO – Carbon Monoxide
  • PM10 and PM2.5 – Particulate Matter
  • O3 – Ozone
  • BTX – Benzene, Toluene, Xylene

Urban traffic stations tend to report higher levels of NO2 and BTX, while SO2 and PM2.5 are generally lower across most locations.

3. Altitude and Location

Station altitudes range from 601 to 730 meters. For example:

  • Plaza de España: 637 m
  • Plaza Castilla: 730 m

Elevation may influence pollutant dispersion, particularly for ozone (O3) in open and elevated areas.

4. Key Trends

  • High-traffic zones (UT) show elevated concentrations of NO2 and BTX.
  • Suburban and park areas (S, UF) exhibit lower pollutant levels, making them safer for sensitive populations.
  • PM10 and PM2.5 levels increase near major avenues and large plazas.
  • Ozone (O3) concentrations are higher in open, suburban areas due to photochemical reactions and reduced traffic emissions.

5. Summary Insight

This dataset enables the identification of high-pollution zones and their correlation with road type and urban density. It also provides a foundation for temporal analysis if additional date-stamped records are included.

Resource Naming Strategy

1. Station Identifiers

  • SHORT_CODE: Unique short identifier, e.g., mad-air-04.
  • STATION_ID: Unique numeric code for the station, e.g., 4.
  • Convention: ST{STATION_ID}_{SHORT_CODE} → ST4_mad-air-04.

2. Location Columns

  • AUTONOMOUS_COMMUNITY: Name of the autonomous community in plain text.
  • autonomous_community_wikidata_uri: Link to Wikidata.
  • PROVINCE, province_wikidata_uri, MUNICIPALITY, municipality_wikidata_uri: Plain text and Wikidata link.
  • ADDRESS: Street or square.
  • LONGITUDE_ETRS89, LATITUDE_ETRS89, LONGITUDE, LATITUDE: Coordinates in decimal degrees.
  • ALTITUDE: Meters above sea level.

3. Station Type Columns

  • TYPE_CODE: Abbreviation for the station type (UT, UF, S).
  • TYPE_NAME: Full name of the type (Urban traffic, Urban background, Suburban).

4. Pollutants

  • NO2, SO2, CO, PM10, PM2_5, O3, BTX: Pollutant concentrations.
  • Optional prefix for derived data:
    • avg_NO2 → average
    • max_PM10 → maximum value

5. Street and Address

  • STREET_CODE, STREET_CLASS, STREET_NUMBER, STREET_NAME: Combination of columns to name the street or square.
  • Concatenation convention: {STREET_CLASS}_{STREET_NUMBER}_{STREET_NAME} → PLAZA_DE_ESPAÑA.

6. Dates

  • Start date: ISO 8601 format YYYY-MM-DDTHH:MM:SSZ.
  • Example: 1998-01-12T00:00:00Z.

7. ETRS89 Coordinates

  • COORD_X_ETRS89, COORD_Y_ETRS89: Numeric values in meters.

8. General Rules

  • Separate words with underscores (_) in column names or derived resources.
  • Avoid special characters in identifiers (e.g., Plaza de España → Plaza_de_Espana).
  • Maintain consistency between IDs, names, and Wikidata links.
  • Optional prefixes to indicate data type: raw_, derived_, avg_, max_.
