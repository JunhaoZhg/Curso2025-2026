Group 08 – Analysis and Resource Naming Strategy

1. Dataset Analysis

Selected datasets

  • Air Quality in Barcelona – measurements of pollutants (NO₂, PM₁₀, etc.) from city monitoring stations.
  • Traffic Street Sections Congestion – information about traffic intensity.
  • Bike Parking Service in Barcelona – locations of public bike parking points.

Data structure and types

DatasetMain columnsNotes
Air quality station_id, station_name, district, long/lat, pollutant, value, datetime Numeric values for pollutant concentration; time-series data.
Traffic section_id, street, start and end points, district, intensity Mobility congestion with its start and end points.
Bike parking id, address, district, lat, lon, type Static dataset with spatial coordinates.

3. Resource Naming Strategy

The following URI structure will be used for both the ontology and the generated RDF data:

TypeBase URI patternExample
Ontology http://bcn-mobility-air.org/ontology/ http://bcn-mobility-air.org/ontology/
Resources http://bcn-mobility-air.org/data/{class}/{identifier} http://bcn-mobility-air.org/resource/Station/1
  • Domain: bcn-mobility-air.org
  • Ontology pattern: slash URIs (/)
  • Data pattern: slash URIs (/)
  • Content negotiation: .html for human view, .ttl for RDF data.

4. Summary

The datasets are well structured, open, and suitable for Linked Data publication. The defined URI strategy ensures unique, persistent identifiers for classes and resources, allowing future linking with external sources such as DBpedia or Wikidata.
